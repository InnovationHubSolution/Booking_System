## 4. Tourism Regulations

### Tourism Act 2019 - Key Provisions

**Licensing Requirements:**

**Tier 1: Accommodation Providers**
- Hotels, resorts, guesthouses with 5+ rooms
- **License Fee:** 150,000 VUV/year
- **Requirements:**
  - Fire safety systems
  - Emergency exits
  - First aid facilities
  - Trained staff
  - Minimum quality standards

**Tier 2: Small Accommodation**
- Guesthouses, B&Bs with <5 rooms
- **License Fee:** 50,000 VUV/year
- **Requirements:**
  - Basic safety standards
  - Clean water supply
  - Waste disposal
  - Privacy measures

**Tier 3: Tour Operators**
- Land tours, boat tours, cultural experiences
- **License Fee:** 100,000 VUV/year
- **Requirements:**
  - Qualified guides

  - Emergency procedures

- Adventure sports, water activities, diving
- **License Fee:** 75,000 VUV/year + activity-specific permits
- **Requirements:**

## 5. Tax Obligations

### Tax System Overview

**No Income Tax!** ðŸŽ‰
- Vanuatu has no personal or corporate income tax
- No capital gains tax
- No withholding tax
- No inheritance tax

**Applicable Taxes:**

**1. Value Added Tax (VAT)**
- **Rate:** 15% (previously 12.5%, increased 2023)
- **Registration Threshold:** 4,000,000 VUV annual revenue
- **Collection:** On goods and services
- **Filing:** Quarterly returns
- **Deadline:** 28th of month following quarter end

**VAT Treatment for Platform:**
```
Service Commission (12%): Subject to VAT
Payment Processing Fee: Subject to VAT
Accommodation Booking: Exempt (host responsibility)
Tour Services: Subject to VAT (if host VAT-registered)
```

**2. Stamp Duty**
- Land transfers: 5% of property value
- Share transfers: 0.3% of share value
- Mortgages: 0.3% of secured amount

**3. Business License Fee**
- Annual fee based on location and business type
- Port Vila: 20,000-100,000 VUV
- Provincial centers: 10,000-50,000 VUV

**4. Departure Tax**
- 5,000 VUV per person leaving Vanuatu
- Usually included in air tickets

### Platform Tax Responsibilities

**As Facilitator:**
1. Charge VAT on platform fees (15%)
2. Issue tax invoices
3. File quarterly VAT returns
4. Maintain tax records (7 years)
5. Remit VAT to VRCA

**Not Responsible For:**
- Host's income tax (none in Vanuatu)
- Guest's personal tax obligations
- Host's VAT on bookings (host's responsibility)

**Example Calculation:**
```
Booking Amount: 25,000 VUV (paid by guest to host)
Platform Fee (12%): 3,000 VUV
VAT on Platform Fee (15%): 450 VUV
-------------------------------------------
Guest Pays: 25,000 VUV (to host) + 3,000 VUV (platform fee) + 450 VUV (VAT) = 28,450 VUV
Host Receives: 25,000 VUV
Platform Receives: 3,000 VUV
Government Receives: 450 VUV (VAT)
```

### Host Tax Obligations

**VAT-Registered Hosts (>4M VUV/year):**
- Charge 15% VAT on accommodation/services
- Issue tax invoices to guests
- File quarterly VAT returns
- Remit VAT to VRCA

**Small Hosts (<4M VUV/year):**
- Not required to register for VAT
- No VAT charged
- Should keep income records
- Consider voluntary VAT registration for credibility

**Record Keeping:**
- Income and expense records: 7 years
- Tax invoices and receipts
- Bank statements
- Booking confirmations
- Platform payout records

## 6. Data Protection & Privacy

### Data Protection Act 2021

**Overview:**
Vanuatu's Data Protection Act 2021 regulates the collection, use, storage, and disclosure of personal information. It's influenced by GDPR but tailored to Vanuatu's context.

**Key Principles:**

**1. Lawful Collection**
- Collect data only for legitimate purposes
- Obtain consent from users
- Provide clear privacy notice

**2. Data Minimization**
- Collect only necessary data
- Don't request excessive information
- Justify each data field

**3. Purpose Limitation**
- Use data only for stated purpose
- Don't repurpose without new consent
- Clear communication of use

**4. Accuracy**
- Keep data accurate and up-to-date
- Allow users to correct information
- Regular data reviews

**5. Storage Limitation**
- Retain data only as long as necessary
- Define retention periods
- Secure deletion procedures

**6. Security**
- Implement appropriate safeguards
- Encryption for sensitive data
- Access controls
- Regular security audits

**7. Accountability**
- Demonstrate compliance
- Maintain records
- Appoint Data Protection Officer (if required)

### Personal Data Categories

**Basic Personal Data:**
- Name, email, phone
- Address, date of birth
- Profile photo
- Nationality

**Sensitive Personal Data (Extra Protection):**
- Payment information
- ID documents (passport, license)
- Health information
- Biometric data

**Business Data:**
- Business name, registration
- Tax ID
- Bank account details

### User Rights

**Right to Access:**
- Users can request all data held
- Provide within 30 days
- Free of charge (first request)

**Right to Rectification:**
- Users can correct inaccurate data
- Update promptly
- Notify third parties if shared

**Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"):**
- Users can request deletion
- Process within 30 days
- Exceptions: legal obligations, ongoing disputes

**Right to Data Portability:**
- Users can export data
- Machine-readable format (JSON, CSV)
- Include all personal data

**Right to Object:**
- Users can opt-out of marketing
- Opt-out of data processing
- Unsubscribe options

### Platform Data Protection Compliance

**Privacy Policy (Required):**
```
Must Include:
â–¡ What data we collect
â–¡ Why we collect it
â–¡ How we use it
â–¡ How long we keep it
â–¡ Who we share it with
â–¡ User rights
â–¡ Contact information for privacy inquiries
â–¡ Cookie policy
```

**Consent Management:**
- Clear opt-in for marketing
- Separate consent for data sharing
- Easy opt-out mechanisms
- Document consent records

**Data Security Measures:**
- Encryption in transit (HTTPS/TLS)
- Encryption at rest (database encryption)
- Secure authentication (JWT, bcrypt)
- Regular security audits
- Access controls (role-based)
- Data breach response plan

**Data Breach Notification:**
- Notify Office of Government CIO: 72 hours
- Notify affected users: Without undue delay
- Document breach details
- Implement corrective measures

**Cross-Border Data Transfers:**
- If storing data outside Vanuatu (AWS, etc.)
- Ensure adequate protection
- Data processing agreements
- Comply with destination country laws

**Data Protection Officer (DPO):**
- Required if processing large amounts of data
- Contact: privacy@vanuatubooking.com
- Oversees compliance
- Handles data subject requests

## 7. Payment Processing Regulations

### Money Laundering and Terrorism Financing Act

**Overview:**
Vanuatu Financial Services Commission (VFSC) regulates financial transactions to prevent money laundering and terrorism financing.

**Platform Requirements:**

**1. Know Your Customer (KYC)**
- Verify user identity
- Document collection: ID, proof of address
- Risk assessment for high-value transactions

**2. Transaction Monitoring**
- Monitor for suspicious activity
- Flag large transactions (>1M VUV)
- Unusual patterns or behavior

**3. Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR)**
- Report suspicious transactions to VFSC
- Timeframe: Within 14 days
- Maintain confidentiality (no "tipping off")

**Suspicious Indicators:**
```
â€¢ Transactions inconsistent with user profile
â€¢ Multiple bookings with different payment cards
â€¢ Immediate cancellations after payment
â€¢ Unusually large bookings
â€¢ Frequent changes to bank account details
â€¢ Reluctance to provide identification
â€¢ Use of multiple accounts
â€¢ Transactions from high-risk countries
```

**4. Record Keeping**
- Transaction records: 7 years
- KYC documents: 7 years
- SAR reports: 7 years

**5. Staff Training**
- Annual AML/CFT training
- Awareness of red flags
- Reporting procedures

### Payment Gateway Compliance

**Licensed Payment Processors:**
- Use licensed payment gateways only
- Ensure PCI-DSS compliance
- Secure payment data handling

**Supported Payment Methods:**
- Credit/Debit cards (Visa, Mastercard)
- Bank transfers (Vanuatu banks)
- International wire
- PayPal (ensure compliance)
- Cryptocurrency (with caution, emerging regulation)

**Payment Data Security:**
- Never store full card numbers
- Use tokenization
- PCI-DSS Level 1 compliance
- Annual security audits

## 11. Employment & Labor Laws

### Employment Act [CAP 160]

**If Platform Employs Staff:**

**Minimum Requirements:**
- Written employment contract
- Minimum wage: 200 VUV/hour (adjusted periodically)
- Maximum 40 hours/week (8 hours/day)
- Overtime: 1.5x regular rate
- Annual leave: 22 working days/year
- Sick leave: 14 days/year (after 6 months)
- Public holidays: Paid time off

**Employee Categories:**

**1. Full-Time Employees**
- Regular hours
- Full benefits
- Written contract

**2. Part-Time Employees**
- Pro-rata benefits
- Written contract

**3. Contractors**
- Fixed-term agreements
- No employment benefits
- Clear scope of work

**Termination Requirements:**
- Notice period: 1 month (standard)
- Severance: 1 week per year of service (if unfair dismissal)
- Final payments: Within 7 days

**Prohibited:**
- Discrimination (gender, race, religion, nationality)
- Child labor (<16 years)
- Forced labor
- Harassment

### Payroll Obligations

**No Income Tax!**
- No withholding required
- Gross = Net pay

**Other Deductions:**
- Vanuatu National Provident Fund (VNPF): 8% employee + 8% employer (if applicable)
- Approved deductions only (loans, insurance, etc.)

**Payroll Records:**
- Maintain for 7 years
- Available for labor inspection

## 14. Risk Management

### Legal Risks & Mitigation

**Risk 1: Unlicensed Hosts**
- **Impact:** Regulatory penalties, platform reputation
- **Mitigation:**
  - Require license uploads during onboarding
  - Annual renewal verification
  - Remove non-compliant listings
  - Cooperate with authorities

**Risk 2: Data Breach**
- **Impact:** Privacy violations, fines, reputation damage
- **Mitigation:**
  - Robust security measures
  - Regular security audits
  - Encryption (transit and rest)
  - Incident response plan
  - Cyber insurance

**Risk 3: Payment Fraud**
- **Impact:** Financial losses, chargebacks
- **Mitigation:**
  - KYC verification
  - Transaction monitoring
  - Fraud detection algorithms
  - Chargeback management
  - Insurance coverage

**Risk 4: Guest/Host Disputes**
- **Impact:** Legal liability, negative reviews
- **Mitigation:**
  - Clear TOS and policies
  - Mediation process
  - Documentation requirements
  - Limited platform liability clause
  - Insurance recommendations

**Risk 5: Safety Incidents**
- **Impact:** Injury claims, lawsuits, reputation
- **Mitigation:**
  - Host insurance requirements
  - Safety standard enforcement
  - Incident reporting system
  - Emergency response procedures
  - Platform liability limitations in TOS

**Risk 6: Tax Non-Compliance**
- **Impact:** Fines, penalties, business closure
- **Mitigation:**
  - Engage qualified accountant
  - Timely VAT filing
  - Accurate record keeping
  - Regular tax reviews
  - Staff training

**Risk 7: Employment Claims**
- **Impact:** Unfair dismissal claims, fines
- **Mitigation:**
  - Written employment contracts
  - Clear policies and procedures
  - Fair treatment
  - Proper termination process
  - Legal consultation for disputes

### Insurance Coverage Summary

**Platform Insurance:**
- Professional indemnity: 10M VUV
- Cyber liability: 5M VUV
- General liability: 5M VUV
- D&O insurance: 10M VUV (if company)

**Host Insurance (Required):**
- Public liability: 5M VUV minimum
